Structure
Level
Beginner sticks to simple combos with sparse movement and body work; each level unlocks more and mixes in more defence and body. Build up starts at beginner in round one and ramps to advanced by the final round (per stance when alternating), layering defence then counters onto the same combos as it climbs — e.g. round 1 calls 1-2-3, later rounds 1-2-3 slip, then 1-2-3 slip 2.

Punches
Movement · your ticked calls
1 jab, 2 cross, 3 lead hook, 4 rear hook, 5/6 uppercuts. Body shots use a b: 1b/2b to the body, 3b/4b body hooks. Drop a slip or roll mid-combo to make it multi-phase, e.g. 1 2 · slip right · 3 2. Build directions as orthodox — left/right flip automatically on southpaw rounds.
Movement
Slips, rolls, steps, pivots and guard work. Ticked ones get woven between your punches, with the punch each slip loads. Directions are shown orthodox and flip automatically on southpaw rounds.
Call style
How punches are spoken. Numbers: "one, two". Names: "jab, cross". Both: "one jab, two cross", and body shots are always called by name ("body hook").
Combo pace
Movement mix
Sets the ceiling for how much defence gets called between punches: slips, rolls, steps, pivots. The Level then scales it down (beginner is sparse). Pick which moves are in play under Combos → Movement.
Body shots
Sets the ceiling for body shots (1b, 2b jab/cross to body, and 3b, 4b body hooks) mixed into freestyle combos. The Level scales it down, so beginner stays light. Named body combos live in Combos → Body.
